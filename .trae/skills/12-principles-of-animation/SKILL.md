# 12 Principles of Animation

This skill provides comprehensive guidance on the 12 principles of animation, which are essential for creating believable and engaging animations.

## The 12 Principles

### 1. Squash and Stretch
The most fundamental principle that gives a sense of weight and flexibility to objects.

### 2. Anticipation
Prepares the audience for a major action, making the action appear more realistic.

### 3. Staging
Directs the audience's attention to the most important elements in a scene.

### 4. Straight Ahead Action and Pose to Pose
Two different approaches to animation timing and planning.

### 5. Follow Through and Overlapping Action
Creates natural movement by showing how different parts of a character or object move at different speeds.

### 6. Slow In and Slow Out
Makes movement appear more natural by gradually accelerating and decelerating.

### 7. Arcs
Most natural movement follows an arc path rather than a straight line.

### 8. Secondary Action
Minor actions that support and enhance the main action.

### 9. Timing
Controls the speed of an action, which affects the perceived weight and personality of an object or character.

### 10. Exaggeration
Amplifies certain aspects of an animation to make it more dramatic or comedic.

### 11. Solid Drawing
Ensures that objects and characters maintain consistent form and volume throughout an animation.

### 12. Appeal
Makes characters and animations visually engaging and likable.

## Application

These principles can be applied to various forms of animation, including 2D, 3D, stop motion, and even UI/UX animations.

## Best Practices

- Start with the basics: master squash and stretch before moving on to more complex principles
- Observe real-world motion for reference
- Use anticipation to build tension and make actions more satisfying
- Pay attention to timing to convey the right mood and weight
- Don't overuse any single principle - balance is key
